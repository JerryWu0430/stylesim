# StyleSim: Business Plan
## Fashion Collection Simulation Platform

---

## Executive Summary

Fashion brands invest significant resources in developing and manufacturing new clothing collections, yet decisions about which designs to produce are often based on intuition, limited trend reports, or historical sales data. This uncertainty frequently leads to overproduction, unsold inventory, and costly discounting.

**StyleSim** addresses this challenge by creating a simulation environment for fashion collections, allowing brands to evaluate and test designs before committing to production.

**Key metrics:**
- TAM: $8B (fashion analytics market)
- SAM: $500-800M (simulation tools)
- Y3 ARR target: $10M
- Pre-Seed ask: $500K

```
│  Design → Produce → Hope it sells → 30-40% doesn't →       │
│  Markdown → Landfill/Incineration                          │

│  Annual cost to industry: $500B+ in unsold inventory       │
│  Environmental: 92M tons textile waste/year                 │

```

| Pain Point | Impact | Who Feels It |
|------------|--------|--------------|
| Gut-based design decisions | 35% avg unsold inventory | Merchandising, Finance |

| Overproduction | 20-40% margin erosion from markdowns | CFO, Sustainability |
| Trend reports ≠ actionable | $50K+ spent on reports nobody uses | Strategy |

**StyleSim:** Simulate your collection before production

| Feature | Benefit |
|---------|---------|
| **Design scoring** | Rate each SKU on predicted sell-through |

## 3. Industry Analysis

### Competitive Landscape

| Player | What They Do | Gap |
|--------|--------------|-----|
| **EDITED** | Retail intelligence, competitor tracking | Descriptive, not predictive |
| **Trendalytics** | Trend forecasting from social/search | No collection-level simulation |
| **Heuritech** | AI trend detection from images | Trend-focused, not merchandising |
| **Stylumia** | Demand sensing | Limited to existing products |
| **Internal teams** | Excel + intuition | No AI, no scale |

**StyleSim differentiation:** End-to-end simulation from design → production decision

### Porter's Five Forces

| Force | Intensity | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| **Rivalry** | Medium | Fragmented; no dominant simulation player |
| **New entrants** | High | Low barriers; big tech could enter |
| **Substitutes** | Medium | Status quo (intuition) is main substitute |
| **Buyer power** | Medium | Large brands have leverage; SMBs don't |
| **Supplier power** | High | Data providers have pricing power |

### Key Success Factors

1. Prediction accuracy >70% correlation to actual sales
2. Integration with existing PLM/ERP workflows
3. Fashion domain expertise (not just tech)
4. Proprietary training data moat

## Unresolved Questions

1. **Accuracy threshold** - what % prediction accuracy needed for buyer confidence?
2. **Data cold start** - bootstrap models with synthetic/public data or need anchor customer?
3. **Integration depth** - light API vs deep PLM embed? Trade-off: speed vs stickiness
4. **Pricing validation** - $100K ACV assumption tested?
5. **Founding team** - who's the fashion domain co-founder?
6. **Competitive moat** - if EDITED builds this in 12mo, what's defensible?
7. **Pilot incentives** - equity/discount/free for data rights?
